  • From the Better User Experience Through Storytelling Articles: How can framing your project in a story format help you refine your ideas? Write a paragraph explanation.

    • Framing a project in a story format refines ideas by grounding abstract goals in a human-centered narrative. Storytelling defines the user as the “hero,” clarifies their journey, and highlights the emotional and strategic goals at stake. This approach unites teams around a shared vision, turning scattered data or requirements into a clear direction. By focusing on the user’s story, designers build empathy and ensure solutions feel purposeful and cohesive before design work even begins.

  • From Meet Your Type: A Field Guide to Love and Typography: What are different ways to establish hierarchy (or emphasis) in typography design? Provide a link to a good example of hierarchy.

    • Designers can create typographic hierarchy by manipulating size, weight, color, style, and spacing. Increasing the size of key text (like headlines) naturally draws the eye, while bold or heavier weights add visual importance. Color can highlight or soften elements, making vibrant or high-contrast hues stand out, while muted tones recede. Playing with styles such as italics, uppercase, or small caps, adds another layer of nuance. And thoughtful spacing or proximity (adjusting margins, line height, and grouping) helps guide the reader through an intentional flow.

Creative Brief

Problem Home cooks often want to share their recipes and unique cooking ideas online, but many existing platforms are overcrowded, lack personalization, or don’t foster real community engagement. As a result, great recipes get lost in the noise, and users don’t always feel valued or connected.
Audience Primary: Home cooks, food enthusiasts, and hobby chefs aged 18–45 who enjoy creating, experimenting, and sharing recipes.
Secondary: Food bloggers, cultural food storytellers, and families who want to preserve and pass down recipes.
Competition
  • AllRecipes & Food.com: High traffic but crowded and impersonal.
  • Cookpad & BigOven: Stronger community feel, but smaller reach.
  • Tasty & Yummly: Popular for discovery, but limited recipe ownership for creators.
Key Messages
  • Recipes deserve to be seen, shared, and celebrated.
  • The platform makes it easy to share cooking ideas with a community that cares.
  • Food connects people, inspiring others, encouraging feedback, and preserving culinary creations.
Call to Action
  • For creators: Upload your recipe today and share your unique flavor with the world.
  • For readers/cooks: Join the community to discover, try, and celebrate recipes from real home cooks.
How to Measure Success
  • Number of new recipe uploads per month.
  • Growth in registered community members.
  • Engagement metrics: likes, comments, shares, and saves.
  • User retention: how often creators return to upload new recipes.
  • Traffic from search and social shares.
