Creative Brief

Problem Home cooks often want to share their recipes and unique cooking ideas online, but many existing platforms are overcrowded, lack personalization, or don’t foster real community engagement. As a result, great recipes get lost in the noise, and users don’t always feel valued or connected.
Audience Primary: Home cooks, food enthusiasts, and hobby chefs aged 18–45 who enjoy creating, experimenting, and sharing recipes.
Secondary: Food bloggers, cultural food storytellers, and families who want to preserve and pass down recipes.
Competition
  • AllRecipes & Food.com: High traffic but crowded and impersonal.
  • Cookpad & BigOven: Stronger community feel, but smaller reach.
  • Tasty & Yummly: Popular for discovery, but limited recipe ownership for creators.
Key Messages
  • Recipes deserve to be seen, shared, and celebrated.
  • The platform makes it easy to share cooking ideas with a community that cares.
  • Food connects people, inspiring others, encouraging feedback, and preserving culinary creations.
Call to Action
  • For creators: Upload your recipe today and share your unique flavor with the world.
  • For readers/cooks: Join the community to discover, try, and celebrate recipes from real home cooks.
How to Measure Success
  • Number of new recipe uploads per month.
  • Growth in registered community members.
  • Engagement metrics: likes, comments, shares, and saves.
  • User retention: how often creators return to upload new recipes.
  • Traffic from search and social shares.
